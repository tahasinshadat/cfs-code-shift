Big Tech

Trust First,
Speed Second

“AWS isn’t racing to be first in AI. It’s trying to be the most trusted.”
— Tahasin Shadat, Episode 1

At Amazon, AI tools spread through word of mouth, not corporate mandates. The training is about what not to do. Engineers are kept on controlled, slightly inferior internal tools to protect sensitive data. Policy first, access second, innovation third.

Private Equity

Speed as
Strategy

“We can’t get to all the things that we need to do fast enough.”
— Jack Harnett, Brookfield

From $600B to over $1T in six years. Brookfield is growing at a pace that demands AI adoption. ChatGPT Enterprise is their most-used application. The struggle isn’t adoption—it’s prioritization.

The Turning Point

The iPhone
Moment

“We’re in the iPhone moment when people were hanging on to BlackBerry.”
— Jack Harnett, Brookfield

Brookfield didn’t discover AI usage through a rollout plan. They discovered it because employees were expensing ChatGPT on corporate credit cards. The revolution isn’t coming—it arrived while no one was looking.

The Big Idea

Scalability
for Thinking

“Code scales deterministic processes. AI scales reasoning. That literally couldn’t be scaled before.”
— John Steer, Brookfield

Traditional software runs the same operation a million times. AI scales something fundamentally different: thinking. An analyst’s reasoning about a pitch deck can now be replicated across hundreds of deals simultaneously.

Academia

Freedom
Without a Playbook

“Education needs to embrace AI, not fight it. But it’s probably making us less capable at reasoning independently.”
— John Steer, Brookfield

No corporate policy. No security training. No legal sign-off. In CS 338 at Northwestern, the tools were available and the guardrails were optional. The result? Extraordinary output—but a looming question about depth of understanding.

The Thesis

These worlds
are converging

As AI becomes more capable, the institutional walls between Big Tech, finance, and education start to look arbitrary. The concerns are already overlapping. The governance frameworks will follow. The question isn’t if—it’s when.
